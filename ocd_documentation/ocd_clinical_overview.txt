OCD — clinical overview (for RAG retrieval; not a substitute for professional care)

Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) involves unwanted intrusive thoughts, images, or urges (obsessions) and/or repetitive behaviors or mental acts (compulsions) aimed at reducing distress or preventing a feared outcome.

Common themes include contamination, symmetry/order, harm, sexual or religious concerns, and “not just right” sensations. Functional impairment and time spent on rituals are key clinical considerations.

Evidence-based treatments
- Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), especially Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP), is a first-line psychological treatment.
- Response prevention means resisting rituals while anxiety is tolerated and habituates over time.
- Medication decisions belong to a qualified prescriber; this document does not give dosing or drug advice.

Safety
- If there is imminent risk of self-harm or harm to others, seek emergency services or local crisis resources immediately.
